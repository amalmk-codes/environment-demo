1. Environmental Ethics

Definition:

Study of how humans should relate morally to nature. Guides decisions affecting animals, plants, ecosystems, and future generations.

Key theories:

  • Anthropocentrism: humans are central; nature is for people.
  • Biocentrism: all living beings have value.
  • Ecocentrism: ecosystems/nature as a whole have value.

2. Sustainable Engineering

Definition:

Design and build solutions that meet present needs while protecting future resources.

Core concepts:

  • Triple Bottom Line: economic, social, environmental sustainability.
  • Life Cycle Analysis: check impacts from production to disposal.
  • Metrics: carbon footprint, water footprint, energy use.

3. Ecosystems & Biodiversity

Basics:

  • Ecosystem = community of organisms + environment.
  • Biodiversity = variety of life; provides food, medicine, resilience.
  • Human impacts: deforestation, pollution, urban sprawl → biodiversity loss.
  • Kerala examples: Western Ghats forests, mangroves, backwaters.

4. Landscape & Urban Ecology

  • Landscape ecology studies spatial patterns and how they affect ecological processes.
  • Urbanization causes habitat loss, heat islands, pollution.
  • Sustainable urban planning: green roofs, parks, rainwater harvesting, public transport.
